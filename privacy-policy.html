Privacy Policy

The Sports Bulletin (“we,” “our,” or “us”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your data when you visit thesportsbulletin.net, subscribe to our newsletter, or interact with our content. By using our website, you agree to the practices described below.

Information We Collect

We collect information to improve our coverage and deliver a better experience for readers. This may include:

  • Personal Information You Provide: When you subscribe to our newsletter, contact us via email, or fill out a form, we may collect your name and email address.
  • Usage Data: We collect anonymized information about how you interact with the site, including pages visited, time on site, device type, browser type, and referral source.
  • Cookies & Tracking: Like most websites, we use cookies and similar technologies to enhance user experience, personalize content, and analyze traffic patterns.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide news, analysis, and updates about the NFL.
  • Send newsletters and communications you opt into.
  • Improve site functionality and tailor content to reader interests.
  • Monitor website performance and security.
  • Comply with legal obligations where required.

Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share anonymized data with trusted service providers for analytics, email delivery, and site performance monitoring. These providers are contractually required to protect your data and use it only for the services we request.

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Cookies are small files stored on your device. We use them to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings.
  • Deliver relevant content and measure performance.
  • Analyze aggregate traffic data to improve site usability.

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings, but some features of the site may not function properly if cookies are turned off.

Third-Party Services

The Sports Bulletin uses analytics tools such as Google Analytics to understand site traffic and improve user experience. These tools may collect anonymized information about your device, browser, and on-site behavior.

We may also include affiliate links to ticketing, merchandise, or fantasy platforms. Clicking these links may allow partners to track purchases for commission purposes.

Email Communications

If you subscribe to our newsletter, we will use your email address to send you curated NFL news, game previews, fantasy football updates, and other relevant content. You can unsubscribe at any time by clicking the “unsubscribe” link at the bottom of any email.

Data Retention & Security

We retain personal information only as long as necessary to provide our services or comply with legal requirements. We use industry-standard security measures to protect your data, but no online transmission is 100% secure.

Children’s Privacy

The Sports Bulletin does not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under 13 years of age. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

International Users

If you are accessing the site from outside the United States, please note that your data may be processed in a country with privacy laws different from your own. By using our site, you consent to this transfer and processing.

Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to the data we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate personal data.
  • Request deletion of your personal data.
  • Withdraw consent for email marketing or data processing.

To exercise these rights, contact us at info@thesportsbulletin.net

Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
